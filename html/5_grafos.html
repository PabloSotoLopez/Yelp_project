The degree centrality of a node is a measure of the node's importance or influence in the graph. It is calculated as the number of edges that are incident to the node. In a social network, a person with a high degree centrality may be considered influential because they have many connections to other people.

The degree centrality of a node can be computed by taking the sum of the degrees of all the nodes in the graph and dividing it by the total number of nodes in the graph minus one. This gives a value between 0 and 1, with higher values indicating a higher degree of centrality.

We select the 4000 most connected nodes as influencers in our network. Afther that, we filter the subgraph only with this nodes.

Then, we make visualitations and community detection by Louvain algorithm.

Louvain algorithm.¶

The Louvain algorithm is a greedy optimization algorithm that is used to identify communities in a graph. It works by iteratively optimizing a metric called modularity, which measures the density of connections within communities compared to connections between communities.

The algorithm starts by assigning each node in the graph to its own community, and then it iteratively merges pairs of communities if doing so increases the modularity of the graph. It stops when it is no longer possible to increase the modularity by merging communities.
